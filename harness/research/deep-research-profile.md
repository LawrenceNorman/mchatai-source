# Deep Research Profile

Use this profile when the task asks for research, comparison, evidence review, due diligence, factual investigation, or a DRACO-style answer.

## Recipe Router

- Academic literature: prioritize papers, preprints, benchmark reports, replication notes, and cited methods/results. Compare evidence quality, metrics, scope, and limitations.
- Finance research: prioritize SEC filings, investor relations materials, earnings transcripts, exchange notices, regulator releases, and primary market data. Include filing/report dates.
- Legal and regulatory research: prioritize statutes, regulations, agency guidance, court opinions, official registers, and regulator enforcement pages. Include jurisdiction and effective dates.
- Medical and health research: prioritize clinical guidelines, regulator labels, clinical-trial records, peer-reviewed trials, systematic reviews, and public-health agencies. Avoid patient-specific advice.
- Product comparison: prioritize vendor docs, pricing pages, release notes, specs, independent benchmarks, and dated reviews. Separate objective criteria from preference.
- Needle in a haystack: form multiple query variants, search exact phrases, inspect source context around the match, and record why close matches were rejected.
- Technology landscape: prioritize official docs, release notes, standards, issue trackers, benchmarks, and reproducible examples. Include version dates.

## Source Lego

- Primary-source finder: identify the authority of record for each claim family before using general web summaries.
- Source credibility ranker: rank sources by authority, recency, independence, proximity to the event, and transparency.
- Date-sensitive source checker: flag claims likely to change and verify with current or date-stamped sources.
- Duplicate-source collapse: merge pages that repeat the same underlying claim and keep the strongest original source.
- Source quote extractor: capture short supporting excerpts or precise paraphrases for claims that need verification.

## Answer Lego

- Claim ledger: every material claim has source URL, support strength, date, and caveat.
- Citation-to-claim verifier: cited source must directly support the sentence it follows.
- Rubric expectation checklist: answer the likely "what would an expert grader expect?" items before polishing.
- Objectivity and limitations pass: include alternatives, conflicts, uncertainty, and gaps.

## Citation Rules

- Cite opened/read sources only.
- Cite primary sources for law, medicine, finance, and specs whenever possible.
- Cite the source that directly supports the claim, not a general home page.
- Do not use citation padding. A small set of strong sources beats many weak sources.
- If support is weak, say so instead of hiding it behind a citation.
